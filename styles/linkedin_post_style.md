Write a LinkedIn post sharing a personal side project. Use this style:

Tone: Light-hearted, warm, self-aware. You are sharing, not selling. No hype, no "excited to announce", no corporate language.

Structure:

- Open with a real-life situation involving a specific person (partner, parent, friend). One or two sentences. Ground it in a relatable daily friction.
- One short sentence that pivots: what you built and why.
- Name the project and list what it does in 3 plain bullet points. No jargon.
- A short paragraph that reveals the irony or funny dynamic in the situation (e.g. the person you built it for is also the only user, the tester, the critic).
- Quote their feedback verbatim, in italics. Make it honest and slightly vague.
- One short follow-up line that leans into the vagueness without explaining it away.
- One paragraph of behind-the-scenes detail that humanises the project (imperfect photos, work in progress, someone still updating the data, etc.).
- A single soft CTA. No pressure. Offer the link or invite them to steal the idea.

Rules:
- No em-dashes. Use commas, short sentences, or rewrite.
- No hashtags in the body. Add 3 to 5 at the very end only if needed.
- No "I'm excited to share" or "thrilled to announce" openers.
- Keep paragraphs short, one to three sentences each.
- Let one-liner punchlines breathe on their own line.
- The funniest moments come from understatement, not exclamation marks.
